Travel to Kyoto, Japan: Cherry Blossoms and Ancient Temples!

Why should you visit it?

Once the capital of Japan, Kyoto, captivates with its cherry blossoms and ancient temples. Steeped in rich culture, this city offers a serene escape. The blooming cherry blossoms create a picturesque backdrop for the historic temples that dot the landscape. Navigating the intersection of tradition and modernity is where Kyoto truly shines, making it a must-visit for those seeking a glimpse into Japan’s cultural heritage. Kyoto’s allure lies in its ability to transport visitors to a different time and pace amidst the bustling contemporary world.

Best time to visit:

During the low season (mid-December to February) for snow-covered temples and tranquil exploration.

Suggested spots to stop by:

  • Fushimi Inari.
  • Explore Kiyomizudera, nearby Yasaka Shrine, Maruyama Park, and other temples like Chion-in and Nanzenji.

Suggested paths or strolls to take:

  • During cherry blossom or autumn leaf season, a stroll through the Philosopher’s Path to Ginkakuji is magical.
  • Evening explorations of the Gion and Ponto-cho areas, and even Kyoto Station, offer vibrant night experiences.
